GibFlow launches on iOS and Android later this year. The business directory is being built now, ahead of the public launch, and early listings are available before the app goes live.
</p>

<p>
The app brings bus routes, cycle parking, tourist routes and local amenities together on one map. That combination is deliberate. A person checking when the next bus arrives at the Town Centre stop, or looking for somewhere to lock a bike near Casemates, is already in the right part of Gibraltar at the right time. The question of where to eat or what to do next is right there. Getting listed puts you in the answer.
</p>

<h2>Why being listed early matters</h2>

<p>
Early listings are not just earlier. They shape the catalogue. The category filters, the proximity results, the suggested places people see when they first open the app — all of it is populated by what is already in the system. Businesses that come in at this stage will be there from day one, not added to an existing directory that people have already formed habits around.
</p>

<p>
GibFlow listings are not advertising slots. There is no bidding, no promoted placement. A restaurant 100 metres from a user shows up before one that is 500 metres away. That is the extent of the ranking. Your position is based on where you are, not what you spend.
</p>

<p>Your listing includes:</p>

<ul>
<li>Location pin and business profile on the map</li>
<li>Category search results — restaurants, pharmacies, hotels, car parks, attractions and more</li>
<li>Proximity results shown to users who are nearby</li>
<li>Search analytics: how many people found you, what they searched, where they were</li>
</ul>

<p>
26,800 people cross the frontier into Gibraltar each day. A large share of them are looking for somewhere to eat, a place to stay, or a service they need right now. GibFlow is how they find it.
</p>

<h2>What sits alongside your listing</h2>

<p>
GibFlow is not a standalone directory. Your business listing sits in the same app as the bus timetables, the real-time route information, the cycle lane map, the secure bike parking locations, and the tourist information layer. That context changes what a listing is worth. Someone using the app to navigate Gibraltar is already looking at the map. Your business is already on it.
</p>

<p>
The types of business we are actively listing now cover most of Gibraltar's retail, hospitality and tourism sectors: restaurants, cafes, bars, hotels, guesthouses, serviced apartments, pharmacies, supermarkets, clothing and specialist retail, hairdressers, beauty and fitness, tour operators, attractions, car parks, and vehicle hire. If people search for it when they are in Gibraltar or planning a visit, it belongs on GibFlow.
</p>
